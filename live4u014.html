The real meaning behind recurring dreams. Do you often dream about your teeth falling out one by one, clattering as they hit the floor? This is an example of a recurring dream, a dream that repeats over time, sometimes for months or longer. Experts believe such dreams reflect unresolved stress, fear, or concerns from our waking lives. Recurring dreams usually involve situations where we feel anxious or powerless. However, they don't have one universal meaning. Dreaming about falling, for instance, might indicate a fear of failure for one person, but a loss of control for another.

Triggers for recurring dreams can come from various sources. Psychologically, stress from family problems can bring about dreams of failing tests. Biologically, conditions like sleep apnea might trigger dreams of drowning or choking. Even environmental factors can play a role, with factors like loud noises or strong smells working their way into the dream content.

If you experience recurring dreams, try keeping a dream journal or meditating before bed. You could also try the three-column technique. After you wake up from a dream, write down your automatic thought in response to it, the feeling it causes, and then a calmer, alternative thought. By employing these strategies, you can use your recurring dreams to your advantage and cut back on the anxiety they bring.
