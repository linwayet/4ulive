Grand Egyptian Museum opens after two decades of delays. The Grand Egyptian Museum gym in Cairo, Egypt, officially opened after more than two decades of construction and delays caused by the Arab Spring protests, the COVID-19 pandemic, and other regional conflicts. World leaders, including presidents, prime ministers, and members of royalty, assembled near the Pyramids of Giza to celebrate the inauguration of the new institution. The museum, built with more than $1 billion in funding, exhibits treasures from Tutankhamen's tomb and a massive statue of Ramesses II, among other artifacts. It also features a modern pyramid-inspired design that stands in sharp contrast to the older Egyptian museum in downtown Cairo. NEO, the world's first humanoid home robot developed by 1X Technologies, became available for pre-order in the U.S. In late October 2025, with deliveries expected to begin in 2026. Standing about 168 centimeters tall and weighing around 30 kilograms, it can walk, carry items, fold laundry, and learn user routines through an AI-powered app. It is priced at $20,000 or $499 per month through a subscription plan. However, NEO is not entirely autonomous. When it encounters more complicated tasks outside its training data, the robot connects to a remote operator, who views the home through its cameras and temporarily takes control. This setup has sparked privacy concerns. Owners must consent to data collection inside their homes to train NEO's AI capabilities. This raises questions about how much privacy remains when both a robot and a remote human operator enter one's daily life.
